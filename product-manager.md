# Product Manager — Case Exercise

## Context

You are joining a fast-growing AI-driven growth platform.

The product uses AI agents to:

- Capture inbound traffic
- Qualify leads
- Adapt messaging dynamically
- Send structured data directly into client CRMs

The system operates across multiple verticals. Each vertical introduces variation in:

- Customer profiles
- Agent logic
- Qualification criteria
- CRM schemas
- Integration workflows

The product is live with paying customers. Demand is growing.

---

## Current Reality

In recent months:

- Onboarding a new client takes 3–4 weeks.
- Engineering is frequently pulled into manual configuration work.
- Agent behavior varies across clients without clear standardization.
- CRM integrations occasionally break when schemas evolve.
- Internal metrics lack consistency across accounts.
- Teams feel stretched.
- Growth expectations are increasing.

Leadership wants to significantly increase onboarding capacity within the next 90 days — without materially increasing headcount.

You are now responsible for this area.

---

## Your Task

Imagine this is your first month in the role.

Describe how you would approach this situation.

You may structure your response however you believe is most effective.

Your response might include:

- How you frame the problem
- How you reduce ambiguity
- How you identify opportunities
- How you prioritize
- How you define success
- How you align teams

We are not looking for a fixed format.

We are interested in:

- How you think
- How you structure complexity
- How you connect decisions to outcomes
- How you incorporate learning into decision-making
- How you create leverage instead of more work

Assume limited senior engineering capacity.
Assume high urgency.
Assume cross-team tension.

Be clear. Be structured. Be intentional.
